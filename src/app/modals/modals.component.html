Automata celular

Cellular automata are spatially and temporally finite-state discrete computational systems composed of a finite set of cells evolving in parallel at discrete time steps. Cellular automata are abstract structures that can be used as general models of complexity. They can also be used for the study of nonlinear dynamics. Cellular automata are inspired by self-reproducing or self-replicating living organisms.

Conway's Game of life

The Game of Life is not your typical computer game. It is a cellular automaton, and was invented by Cambridge mathematician John Conway. It consists of a grid of cells which, based on a few mathematical rules, can live, die or multiply. Depending on the initial conditions, the cells form various patterns throughout the course of the game.

  1. Isolation
    Each cell with one or no neighbors dies, as if by solitude.
  2. Survival
    Each cell with two or three neighbors survives.
  3. Overpopulation
    Each cell with four or more neighbors dies, as if by overpopulation.
  4. Birth
    Each cell with three neighbors becomes populated.
